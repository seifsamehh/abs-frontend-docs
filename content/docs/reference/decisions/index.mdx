## What are ADRs?

ADRs are short documents that capture important architectural decisions along with their context and consequences. They help teams:

- **Remember** why decisions were made
- **Onboard** new team members quickly
- **Evaluate** past decisions in new contexts
- **Avoid** repeating discussions

## ADR Template

When creating a new ADR, use this template:

```markdown
---
title: "ADR-XXX: [Short Title]"
---

# ADR-XXX: [Title]

## Status

[Proposed | Accepted | Deprecated | Superseded by ADR-XXX]

## Context

[Describe the issue that motivated this decision]

## Decision

[Describe the decision that was made]

## Consequences

### Positive

- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]

### Negative

- [Drawback 1]
- [Drawback 2]

### Neutral

- [Side effect 1]

## References

- [Link to relevant documentation]
- [Link to discussions]
```

---

## How to Propose a New ADR

1. **Create a branch** named `docs/adr-xxx-short-title`
2. **Copy the template** and fill in the details
3. **Submit a PR** for team review
4. **Discuss** in the PR comments
5. **Update status** to "Accepted" once approved
