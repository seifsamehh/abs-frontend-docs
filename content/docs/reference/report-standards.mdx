# üìä Report Standards

<Callout type="info">
  These report templates promote **self-reflection** and **accountability**, key
  best practices in tech performance reviews. They help track progress, identify
  blockers, and plan growth effectively.
</Callout>

## üìã Overview

Weekly reports are essential for:

- **Tracking progress** and accomplishments
- **Identifying blockers** early
- **Planning professional development**
- **Maintaining team transparency**
- **Facilitating effective code reviews**

---

## üë®‚Äçüíª Junior Developer Report Template

Junior developers submit a weekly self-report that encourages reflection on accomplishments, challenges, and growth areas.

### Template Structure

```markdown
# Weekly Self-Report - [Start Date] to [End Date]

**Prepared by:** [Your Name], Junior Frontend Developer  
**Date Submitted:** [Submission Date]

---

## üéØ Accomplishments

[List all completed tasks with specific details, evidence, and measurable impact]

### Example Format:

- **Task:** [Brief description]
  - **Details:** [What you did and how]
  - **Evidence:** [PR link, commit hash, or deployed feature URL]
  - **Metrics/Impact:** [Quantifiable results]

---

## üöß Blockers/Problems

[Describe challenges encountered, how you addressed them, and any ongoing issues]

### Example Format:

- **Blocker:** [Issue description]
  - **Impact:** [How it affected your work]
  - **Resolution:** [Steps taken or help received]
  - **Status:** [Resolved/Ongoing/Escalated]

---

## ‚úÖ Positives

[Highlight strengths demonstrated this week]

### Example Format:

- **Skill/Behavior:** [What you did well]
  - **Context:** [Situation where you applied it]
  - **Impact:** [Positive outcome for team/project]

---

## üìà Areas for Improvement

[Self-identified gaps with honest reflection and constructive suggestions]

### Example Format:

- **Area:** [Skill or knowledge gap]
  - **Why It Matters:** [Impact on your work]
  - **Suggestion:** [How you plan to address it]

---

## üéì Development Plans

[Actionable steps for professional growth with clear timelines and success metrics]

### Example Format:

- **Goal:** [What you want to learn/improve]
  - **Actions:** [Specific steps you'll take]
  - **Timeline:** [Expected completion date]
  - **Success Metric:** [How you'll measure progress]
```

## üìÅ File Naming Convention

<Callout type="warn">
  **Consistent naming is critical** for document management, compliance, and
  team coordination.
</Callout>

### Standard Format

```
Weekly_Performance_Report_[StartDate]_to_[EndDate]_PreparedBy_[Name].pdf
```

### Examples

**Junior Reports:**

```
Weekly_Performance_Report_2025-12-28_to_2026-01-02_PreparedBy_AhmedHassan.pdf
Weekly_Performance_Report_2026-01-04_to_2026-01-09_PreparedBy_FatimaAlSayed.pdf
```

**Coordinator Reports:**

```
Weekly_Team_Report_2025-12-28_to_2026-01-02_PreparedBy_SarahChen.pdf
Weekly_Team_Report_2026-01-04_to_2026-01-09_PreparedBy_MohamedKamal.pdf
```

### Naming Rules

1. **Date Format:** `YYYY-MM-DD` (e.g., 2026-01-04 for January 4, 2026) - ISO 8601 standard for better readability
2. **No Spaces in Names:** Use camelCase for names (e.g., AhmedHassan, not Ahmed Hassan)
3. **Consistent Separator:** Use underscores `_` to separate sections
4. **File Extension:** Always use `.pdf` for final reports
5. **Version Control:** If revisions needed, append `_v2`, `_v3`, etc.

## üéØ What Makes a Great Report

### For Juniors:

‚úÖ **Demonstrates Growth:** Shows learning and improvement over time  
‚úÖ **Provides Context:** Explains not just what you did, but why and how  
‚úÖ **Includes Metrics:** Quantifies impact wherever possible  
‚úÖ **Shows Initiative:** Highlights proactive actions and problem-solving  
‚úÖ **Plans Forward:** Clear development goals with actionable steps  
‚úÖ **Honest Reflection:** Acknowledges challenges and areas to improve

‚ùå **Avoid:**

- Vague statements without examples
- Listing tasks without context or impact
- Hiding blockers or challenges
- Exaggerating accomplishments
- Generic improvement areas without plans

### For Coordinators:

‚úÖ **Comprehensive Team View:** Aggregates individual and team performance  
‚úÖ **Balanced Feedback:** Recognizes strengths, addresses gaps constructively  
‚úÖ **Data-Driven Insights:** Uses metrics to support observations  
‚úÖ **Strategic Planning:** Connects weekly work to larger goals  
‚úÖ **Actionable Next Steps:** Clear priorities and assignments  
‚úÖ **Team Development Focus:** Identifies growth opportunities for team members

‚ùå **Avoid:**

- Rubber-stamping junior reports without verification
- Generic feedback that doesn't help growth
- Focusing only on problems without solutions
- Neglecting your own development in favor of team reporting
- Missing patterns across team members
